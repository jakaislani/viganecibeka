    The best canned cocktails come in a variety of flavors, strengths, and styles. So how to distill the whole of the ever-growing beverage category into one measly little list? Can you even compare a canned Old Fashioned to a canned Margarita? I did my best. And, as a seasoned canned cocktail drinker and lover, you can trust me. 

    Related: 3 Best Canned Ranch Waters

    But first, what is a canned cocktail? The category is much broader than other canned alcoholic beverages. According to a few different state liquor boards, a canned cocktail is simply a canned, pre-mixed drink that is over 0.5% ABV but less than 15% ABV. Beyond that, it’s the Wild West, baby. These days, there are not only canned versions of classic cocktails like Palomas and Old Fashioneds, but there’s also original canned cocktails—new creations Mr. Boston never dreamed of when he was writing up his guide. 

    Related: Best Hard Kombucha You Can Buy

    So, did I try all the canned cocktails on the market? It’s an impossible feat. Instead, I created a curating tasting list of some of the top rated canned cocktails out there, as well as canned mixed drinks that were new to the scene. 
    Over the course of two separate taste tests, I tried 26 different canned cocktails. I sipped each one, judging them on what the cocktail was trying to do—is this canned Margarita a quality Margarita? Does this canned Gin & Tonic taste like a G&T I might make at home? After the first taste test, I found ten canned mixed drinks that stood out amongst the rest. The second taste test resulted in three new additions. Here are the best canned cocktails.

    Best Pool Drink

    Cutwater Pineapple MargaritaTotal Wine

    Cutwater was one of the first widely available canned cocktail brands on the market. And while I will forever be sad that my favorite of their offerings (the wonderfully cucumbery Gin & Tonic) is nearly impossible to find, I appreciate how much they’ve grown and how many Cutwater flavors there are (Long Island Iced Tea? White Russian? Cosmo??). Within that lineup is a selection of Margarita flavors that are impressive in their own right. And out of those Margarita flavors, the pineapple is the best. Unlike many other canned Margaritas, Cutwater does not carbonate their Margaritas. So this tastes more like a traditional take on the cocktail, albeit with less of a distinct tequila flavor. “Like pineapple upside down cake,” managing editor Gwynedd Stuart noted. I like to add a splash of lime to this can. With that little addition, it’s one of the best canned mixed drinks to sip poolside.

    Best Tropical

    Dogfish Head Vodka Crush Blood Orange and MangoDrizlyReserve Bar

    Dogfish Head is primarily known for their somewhat experimental beers (they also make one of the best pumpkin beers). But the Delaware brewery also owns a distillery where they make out-of-the-box spirits like Roasted Peanut Vodka, Palo Santo Whiskey, and Punkin Liqueur, as well as standard gin, vodka, and whiskey. These “Culinary-Crafted Cocktails” are just the latest addition to their boozy lineup. And while we liked some of the other ones, this vodka-spiked blood orange and mango drink was our favorite. There’s tons of fruity mango on the nose, and the drink itself is very tangy with lots of fresh citrusy zest. It’s the best canned cocktail for a beach trip—not too sweet, not too boozy (7%), and appropriately tropical.

    Best Strong

    Golden Rule Old FashionedTotal WineReserve Bar

    Golden Rule mixed bourbon, aromatic bitters, orange, and cane sugar in a teeny can, and I’m so glad they did. It seems simple. It seems like a no-brainer. But a ready-to-drink Old Fashioned is a great idea. And Golden Rule executed the cocktail perfectly—it’s strong (39.5% ABV), it’s sweet, it’s nuanced. And the best part? I don’t have to make simple syrup or muddle bitters with sugar. “Who wants to make an Old Fashioned?” Gwynedd commented. “This is great.”

    Best Craft Cocktail

    Five Drinks Variety PackTotal WineInstacart

    Five Drinks Co.’s goal: Make the best canned cocktails with just five ingredients. Each cocktail in this mixed pack tastes like a craft drink. Each one also has a twist. The Daiquiri, for instance, is made with pineapple and a spiced syrup. The Margarita includes hibiscus and habanero. The Moscow Mule contains tamarind and coconut water. And the Mojito is a passion fruit Mojito. These cans are smaller but they pack more of a punch than most of the drinks on this list, coming in at 9% ABV.

    Best Whiskey, Runner-Up

    Jameson Ginger & LimeTargetInstacart

    This canned cocktail smells like a Whiskey Ginger. It tastes like a Whiskey Ginger. It is, in fact, a Whiskey Ginger. So many canned cocktails are advertised as one thing but taste like another. Jameson has kept things simple, creating a canned version of one of the most popular drinks made with the distillery’s Irish whiskey. But the thing that took it over the top and made it one of the best canned cocktails is the ginger—it’s spicy. This isn’t a Whiskey Ginger Ale, it’s a Whiskey Ginger Beer. Yum.

    Best Coffee Cocktail

    Golden Rule Tini’s Midnight Lounge Espresso MartiniGolden Rule Spirits

    Golden Rule makes a second appearance on the list! This time with their Espresso Martini. Sold in another adorably tiny, beautifully designed can, it’s not a creamy ‘tini. This is a stiff, sophisticated, coffee-forward Espresso Martini. It smells dark and rich and roasted. And the flavor is delightfully balanced—not too sweet, not too strong, with a bitter bite of coffee on the finish. You could absolutely pour this canned cocktail into a Martini glass and fool anyone into thinking you made it from scratch. In my experience, DIY Espresso Martinis are one of the more time intensive cocktails to make at home (you have to brew espresso!), so it’s great this exists. It is one of the best canned cocktails to make your at-home cocktailing much, much easier.

    Best Nightcap

    Hochstadter’s Slow & Low Rock and RyeTotal WineReserve BarDrizly

    Back in the 1800s, this mix of rock candy and rye whiskey was sold as a cure-all. Unfortunately, all it really cured was bad whiskey, which bartenders made more palatable by mixing with sugar and occasionally fruit. This modernized, ready-to-drink version, on the other hand, is made with quality rye (so it tastes even better than its predecessors), along with rock candy, raw honey, navel oranges, and Angostura bitters. It’s a lot like an Old Fashioned, but with a more honey- and fruit-forward flavor. I try to keep a few of these extremely adorable canned cocktails in my fridge at all times for easy nightcaps.

    Best Bloody Mary

    Cutwater Spicy Bloody MaryDrizlyTarget

    It’s not difficult to make a Bloody Mary at home—simply combine Bloody Mary Mix (opt for one of the best Bloody Mary Mixes from our list) with some vodka. But these cans are the best canned cocktail for brunch on the go. Cutwater’s Bloody Mary cans are always at the top of my camping packing list and they’re also great for the beach. Or a hike. Or a fishing trip. Or a couple of steps out your front door. It’s rich and zesty and packed with umami flavors. It’s also the perfect level of thickness—somewhere between tomato juice and tomato soup. It’s the best canned Bloody Mary you can buy—and it comes in different spice levels.

    Best Paloma

    Cazadores PalomaTargetInstacartDrizly

    If you poured this into a highball glass and added some ice and a grapefruit garnish, I would absolutely think a bartender made this Paloma. If you’re one of the many Paloma heads out here, you need to buy a pack of these canned cocktails ASAP. It’s zesty, it’s not too sweet, and it actually tastes like there’s tequila in there—good tequila. If you like both Margs and Palomas, I highly recommend getting the Cazadores Fiesta Pack, which includes the Paloma and the Spicy Margarita along with the standard Margarita, which didn’t make this list but is still quite good—it made our best canned Margarita list.

    Best Gin & Tonic Substitute

    The Finnish Long DrinkWalmartInstacartReserve Bar

    If you want to drink like actor Miles Teller, this is the best canned cocktail for you. Teller, you see, is a huge fan of the Long Drink and one of the people responsible for spreading the word of the Finnish canned mixed drink amongst Americans. Who can blame him for wanting to get involved? This canned cocktail is delicious. Made with citrus soda, gin, and a hint of juniper berry, it’s essentially a spiked Fresca. It’s also a better take on a Gin & Tonic than most canned Gin & Tonics currently on the market. I’m all in on the original, but the company also offers a festive cranberry flavor along with Long Drink Zero, which has zero sugar and just 99 calories, and Long Drink Strong, which comes in at 8.5% ABV (3% higher than Long Drink Traditional).

    Best Whiskey Cocktail

    Jack Daniel’s Coca-ColaTarget

    It’s Jack. It’s Coke. It’s dangerous. It’s pretty simple: If you like Jack & Coke, you’ll like this canned Jack & Coke. Even if you don’t like Jack & Coke, you’ll probably like this canned cocktail. Because Coke is good (in fact, it’s the best cola, per our taste test and ranking). The one thing I’ll say is this could use some dilution—it’s strong with a lot of strong flavors. Pour this into a glass over some pebble ice and, as Coca-Cola will tell you, enjoy.

    Best Spicy Cocktail

    Cazadores Spicy MargaritaTargetDrizly

    A bartender once told me if they wanted a drink to sell, they’d just muddle some jalapeño into it. There’s something about a cocktail with a touch of heat that keeps people coming back for more. And the Spicy Margarita in particular is having a real moment. This canned Margarita has great spice—it has that grassy jalapeño pepper flavor and a level of heat that lets you know the pepper is there but won’t burn your lips. It is fizzy—something a real Margarita rarely is—but that keeps it tasting fresh and crisp in the can. Gwynedd even said, “If I got this at a bar, I would be so happy.” Since tasting it, I have bought it multiple times. It’s the best canned cocktail for heat-seekers for sure and it was the best canned cocktail, period, until we tasted the final entry on this list.

    Best of the Best

    Salt Point GreyhoundTotal Wine

    In my opinion, Salt Point is making some of the best cocktails in a can right now. The brand’s Cape Cod is zingy and tart. The Gin Highball is absolutely gulpable. And the Moscow Mule is delightfully spicy. But the Greyhound is a true mixological feat. It smells like a cocktail garnished with fresh grapefruit zest. And the flavor is so nuanced—a tangy, zesty mix of grapefruit, lime, and vodka. It even tastes like there’s a slight touch of salt. It comes in at 10% ABV and, according to the label, there are two servings per can, so watch out. It’s the best canned cocktail, good enough to unseat the former champion. Seek it out or demand your grocery store get it in stock.
