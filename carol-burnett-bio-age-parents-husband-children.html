    Carol Burnett Bio, Age, Parents, Husband, Children, Net Worth – American actress, comedienne, singer, and writer, Carol Creighton Burnett was born on April 26, 1933.

    Carol Burnett Biography

    She gained fame with her revolutionary comedy variety show, The Carol Burnett Show, which aired on CBS and was one of the first programs hosted by a woman. Burnett is recognized for her versatile performances on stage, television, and film, in both dramatic and comedic roles.

    She has won several prestigious awards, including six Primetime Emmy Awards, a Tony Award, a Grammy Award, and seven Golden Globe Awards.

    In 2005, Carol Burnett was presented with the Presidential Medal of Freedom, and in 2013, she received the Mark Twain Prize for American Humor, followed by the Screen Actors Guild Life Achievement Award in 2015.

    Burnett spent her childhood in San Antonio, Texas, but later moved to California and attended Hollywood High School. She pursued her passion for theater and musical comedy at the University of California, Los Angeles (UCLA), and also performed in nightclubs in New York City.

    In 1959, she made her breakthrough on Broadway with Once Upon a Mattress, earning a Tony Award nomination. Burnett then appeared on The Garry Moore Show and won her first Emmy Award in 1962. Her television special debut was in 1963, starring as Calamity Jane in the Dallas State Fair Musicals production of Calamity Jane on CBS.

    Burnett relocated to Los Angeles and headlined The Carol Burnett Show on CBS television from 1967 to 1978. The show had its roots in vaudeville and blended comedy sketches with music and dance performances.

    Burnett created numerous unforgettable characters during the program’s duration, and both she and the show won multiple Emmy and Golden Globe Awards. After her variety show, Burnett appeared in a variety of television and film projects, including the films Pete ‘n’ Tillie (1972), The Front Page (1974), A Wedding (1978), The Four Seasons (1981), Annie (1982), Noises Off (1992), and Horton Hears a Who! (2008).

    She also gave remarkable performances in dramas like 6 Rms Riv Vu (1974) and Friendly Fire (1979), and guest roles in Mad About You, winning an Emmy Award. She returned to Broadway in 1995 in Moon Over Buffalo, earning another Tony Award nomination. Recently in 2022, she appeared in the AMC drama Better Call Saul.

    Burnett is also an accomplished writer and narrator of several memoirs, earning Grammy nominations for most of them, including In Such Good Company, for which she won a Grammy.

    In 2019, the Golden Globes introduced the Carol Burnett Award in her honor, recognizing her remarkable career in television. Burnett received her first award at the event. On her 90th birthday, NBC presented her with a special program, Carol Burnett: 90 Years of Laughter + Love, celebrating her achievements.
