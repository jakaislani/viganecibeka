    David Schwimmer Bio | Wiki

    David Schwimmer is a popular American actor, director, and producer who got worldwide recognition for portraying Ross Geller in the sitcom Friends, for which he won a Screen Actors Guild Award and a Primetime Emmy Award nomination for Outstanding Supporting Actor in a Comedy Series in 1995.

    While still acting in Friends, David’s first leading film role was in The Pallbearer (1996), followed by roles in Kissing a Fool, Six Days, Seven Nights, Apt Pupil (all 1998), as well as Picking Up the Pieces (2000). He was later cast as Herbert Sobel in the miniseries Band of Brothers (2001). He started began his acting career performing in school plays at Beverly Hills High School. Following graduation, David co-founded the Lookingglass Theatre Company.

    For most of the late 1980s, David lived in Los Angeles as a struggling, unemployed actor, until he starred in the television movie A Deadly Silence in 1989 and appeared in many television roles in the early 1990s, such as L.A. Law, The Wonder Years, NYPD Blue, and Monty. In 2016, David starred as lawyer Robert Kardashian in The People v. O. J. Simpson, for which he won his second Primetime Emmy Award nomination, this time for Outstanding Supporting Actor in a Limited Series or Movie.

    Following the series finale of Friends in 2004, David branched out into film and stage work. David became cast as the title character in the 2005 drama film Duane Hopwood, voiced Melman the Giraffe in the computer-animated Madagascar film franchise, acted in the dark comedy Big Nothing (2006), as well as the thriller Nothing but the Truth (2008). David’s feature film directorial debut followed in 2007 with the comedy Run Fatboy Run, and the next year he made his Off-Broadway directorial debut in Fault Lines.

    David Schwimmer Education

    He attended Beverly Hills High School. David enrolled in a drama class, where he appeared in stage productions. Inspired by his school drama teacher to further his acting, David flew to Chicago for a summer acting program at Northwestern University. In 1984, David graduated from Beverly Hills High and wanted to go straight into acting, but his parents insisted he go to college first so he would have something to fall back on.

    David enrolled in Northwestern University, where he had attended the summer acting program earlier. At the university, David studied theater and was in an improv group with Stephen Colbert, the No-Fun Mud Piranhas. Following graduating in 1988, with a Bachelor of Arts degree in theater and speech, David co-founded the Lookingglass Theatre Company. Later on, David returned to Los Angeles to pursue an acting career.

    David Schwimmer On Friends

    In 1994, David became cast as Ross Geller in NBC’s situation comedy Friends, a series that revolved around a group of friends who live near each other in Manhattan. David played a hopeless-romantic paleontologist who serves at a museum and later becomes a professor at a university. He initially turned down the role as Ross but accepted later. Executive producer Kevin S. Bright said that he had formerly worked with David, the character of Ross was written with him in mind, and he was the first actor cast.
